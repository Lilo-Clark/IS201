AAPL Facts

  1. Apple Inc. (AAPL) went public on December 12, 1980, with an initial public offering (IPO) price of $22 per share.
  2. Steve Jobs, Apple's co-founder, was fired from the company in 1985, and the stock suffered a decline in the years following his departure.
  3. Apple's fortunes turned around in the late 1990s with the return of Steve Jobs, and the company launched several successful products such as the iMac and iPod.
  4. In 2007, Apple released the first iPhone, which revolutionized the smartphone industry and contributed significantly to the company's growth.
  5. AAPL became the first publicly traded U.S. company to reach a $1 trillion market capitalization in August 2018.
  6. Apple's stock split four-for-one on August 31, 2020, which brought down the price of the stock but made it more accessible to retail investors.
  7. During the COVID-19 pandemic, AAPL's stock price initially declined but rebounded strongly as the company's services and products were in high demand due to the shift to remote work and online shopping.
  8. Apple announced in April 2021 that it would increase its share buyback program by $90 billion and raise its dividend by 7%, signaling confidence in its future growth prospects.
  9. As of April 18, 2023, AAPL had a market capitalization of over $2.5 trillion, making it one of the most valuable companies in the world.
  10. Apple's stock has consistently outperformed the broader market over the years, and it is often held by long-term investors as a core holding in their portfolios.
